# Data-Mining-Project

The global telecommunications industry is one of the most dynamic and competitive sectors in the modern economy. With deregulation and technological advancements, customers face minimal barriers to switching service providers, resulting in a phenomenon known as customer churn. This voluntary departure of subscribers represents a critical challenge for telecom companies, leading directly to substantial revenue loss, increased costs for customer acquisition, and erosion of market share. Studies suggest that acquiring a new customer can cost significantly more than retaining an existing one. Therefore, the ability to predict and prevent churn has become a paramount strategic objective for sustaining profitability and long-term viability in this saturated market.

Customer churn is a complex, multi-faceted problem driven by a variety of factors, including service quality, pricing, competitor offers, and customer satisfaction. The challenge lies in identifying at-risk customers before they make the final decision to leave. Without an automated, data-driven system, identifying these subtle indicators across millions of customer records is virtually impossible, leading to reactive and often ineffective retention efforts.
